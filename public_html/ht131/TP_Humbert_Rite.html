Rite of Passage

It's my tenth birthday; Mum and Dad have given permission for me to walk home from school with a friend. My school is two-teacher, rural, and in the wheat belt of western Victoria. It's the final day of term, the beginning of the six-week Christmas holidays. Mum doesn't know my friend is already away visiting her cousins. Walking home by myself, I take the shortcut across the wheat field to the old gumtree in the middle of the paddock. It's shady and inviting under leafy spreading branches. I choose a protruding root to rest on.
