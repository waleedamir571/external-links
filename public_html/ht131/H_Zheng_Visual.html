Visual Shock

Oxford Street, London, 1964. In a shop-window a mannequin in a breast-bare dress attracts two women pedestrians. They can’t believe their eyes. Both place their right hands on their bosoms as if to cover their shock. Another woman who walks by casts a sidelong glance at them.

Life magazine reported that a London architect’s wife bought an evening dress, topless, and wore it to a party. All night long a debate of its merits and demerits continued among partygoers.
