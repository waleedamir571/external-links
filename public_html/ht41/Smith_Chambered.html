Chambered Nautilus

Somewhere in the ocean, a nautilus floats upright. Its origin is a mystery. We have searched for its eggs in the depths and found none. Rather, look within, and discover that the nautilusis its own history. The outer shell curves around casts of its old, smaller bodies. As the creature grows, it seals off a room and moves to a new one, strung along a spiral and always 6.3% bigger than the old. But it does not forget its past places, and it keeps one tendril moored to the first room.
