Woollahra, New South Wales, Australia

 

Stone Circles

The cemetery gate closes behind me with barely a squeak. I tiptoe past the neat mounds. Edged with rough stones, each grave is like a mini Stonehenge. Burial sites have been pegged with rock slabs by grieving parents. The makeshift headstones are unmarked, secured with rusty wire or hemmed by earth. Pennyweight Flatâ€”1852, named for little value. No hope for alluvial fortune in this ground. Only the remains of mites, uncoffined, unregistered. Children cut down by diphtheria, typhoid and dysentery, by a creek polluted from the sluicing. Shaken, I stand in this paddock of bones. The tombs are as small as a newspaper page, tiny as words before the rush.

spring frost
