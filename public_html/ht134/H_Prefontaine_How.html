How It Sometimes Happens

There are moments when art almost attains to the dignity of manual labor.
                                                      – Oscar Wilde

The first image drifts into my consciousness as I chop kindling for the evening fire. Two days later, as I pick the last of my winter squash, a few phrases drop by like new neighbors to say hello. Revision may require days or years, as I pour gravel into potholes, intent on fixing the roads around me.
