Lifting the Veil

We are all sleepwalkers. Blind to reality, we need to be awakened so we can see life as it is. The dust that covers our eyes . . . Is it just a thin film? Can one just blow it away? Or must we rise to Buddha-height to wipe it off?

Seeing the saffron-coloured robe worn by yogis, Kabir says, "Hey, yogi, colour your mind first with love."

spring-cleaning
my entire house . . .
I cling
to each minute detail
of my fights and flights

and when I begin
to run in chaotic circles,
unruffled
the wall clock sticks
to its rhythm
