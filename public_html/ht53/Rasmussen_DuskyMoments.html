Dusky Moments

Free of the Canadian winter, I float into the warm, transparent waters of Saint Croix. On my way to the beach, I had bought snorkel, mask and fins and a copy ofGuide to Marine Life. Yellow-finned Blue Tangs and black-striped Angelfish flow like butterflies through the reef while feeding on algae. A tiny, coal-colored Dusky Damselfish patrols its private patch and drives off invaders by feigning attacks and nipping them.
