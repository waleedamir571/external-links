Until death . . .

"You are a small computer," he said, slowly, clearly. "I am a large computer, and a philosopher. I can contain you, and more." For years, I felt so ashamed for blundering into that marriage. Even later, married again, I would imagine the doorbell ringing. After he killed me, what would he do to my children? It was twenty years before I could speak of him; thirty before I stopped hoping he was dead.
