Inside Out

Your life—now, that’s real. You know because you can feel every detail of it from the inside. The craving for a hot dog, the five extra pounds, the nearsightedness, the scar on your thumb. There’s no ignoring the feel and the fact of you.

The cars on the freeway, though, are only a bunch of metal boxes between you and your destination. The line at the grocery store is just that—a line as impersonal as a point or a circle. Corporations. The government. Everything out there is simply the backdrop against which your own life is taking place.

Klein bottle
and Mobius strip
the inside turns out
